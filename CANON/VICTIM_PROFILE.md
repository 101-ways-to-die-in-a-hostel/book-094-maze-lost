# Victim Profile

## Basic Information

**Full Name:** Kyle MacIntyre
**Nickname/Handle:** @KyleExplores
**Age:** 26
**Nationality:** American (Texas)
**Occupation:** Sales rep (taking extended leave)

## Archetype
**Primary:** The Lone Wolf

**Secondary traits:** Hyper-masculine, tech-averse traveler, aggressively independent

## The Fatal Flaw
**Core Flaw:** Pride

**How it manifests:**
- Refuses to use maps or GPS ("that's for tourists")
- Won't ask for directions under any circumstances
- Believes getting lost is "part of the adventure"
- Mocks other travelers who plan ahead

**How it kills them:**
His stubborn refusal to ask for help or use navigation tools traps him in a labyrinthine hostel where intuition fails and walls don't follow logic.

## Physical Appearance
- **Height/Build:** 6'1", athletic, former college football player going slightly soft
- **Hair:** Sandy brown, perpetually messy
- **Distinguishing features:** Square jaw, cocky grin, tribal tattoo on shoulder he got in Cancun at 19
- **Typical outfit:** Cargo shorts, tank top, flip flops, Patagonia backpack

## Personality

### Positive Traits (that get twisted)
1. Adventurous - becomes reckless disregard for safety
2. Self-reliant - becomes pathological refusal to accept help

### Negative Traits (that seal their fate)
1. Macho pride - won't admit he's lost until it's too late
2. Dismissiveness - ignores local advice as "unnecessary"

### Pet Peeves
- "Tourists" (as opposed to "travelers" like himself)
- People who over-plan

### Fears
- Surface fear: Looking weak or incompetent
- Deeper fear: Being ordinary, being just another tourist

## Background

### Origin Story
Grew up in suburban Houston, youngest of three brothers, constantly competing to prove himself. Dad was a "real man's man" who taught him never to show weakness. Kyle was always trying to earn approval through toughness. Travel is his way of proving he's more adventurous than his brothers.

### Why They Travel
To prove he's not just another guy from the suburbs. To have stories that one-up everyone at parties. To find himself (though he'd never phrase it that way - too soft).

### How They Ended Up at This Hostel
Following a "hidden gems" travel blog that recommended staying in "authentic" medina accommodations. The more confusing the address, the more "real" it felt.

## Voice & Speech

### Vocabulary
- Uses words like: "bro," "epic," "sick," "crushing it," "authentic"
- Avoids words like: "scared," "lost," "help"
- Catchphrases: "I got this" / "GPS is for tourists" / "This is the real experience"

### Speech Pattern
- Loud, confident, interrupts
- Bro-speak mixed with travel blog vocabulary
- Slight Texas drawl he sometimes exaggerates

### Internal Monologue Style
Constantly self-narrating his "epic journey." Defensive, comparing himself favorably to others. Secretly more insecure than he appears.

## Digital Presence

### Social Media
- **Platform of choice:** Instagram (adventure photos)
- **Follower count:** 8,400
- **Posting style:** Shirtless photos at landmarks, "off the beaten path" brags
- **Last post before death:** Photo of hostel courtyard with caption "Lost in the maze of the medina. This is what REAL travel looks like. No Google Maps needed."

### Online vs Reality
Presents himself as a fearless explorer. Reality: carefully chooses "dangerous-looking" spots that are actually safe.

## Relationships

### Family
- Competitive relationship with two older brothers
- Dad who texts "Proud of you, son" only when he posts adventurous content

### Friends
- College buddies he brags to but rarely sees
- Travel acquaintances he competes with

### Romantic
- Recently single after girlfriend said he "cared more about his Instagram than her"

### At the Hostel
- Tries to impress a group of Swedish travelers with his "no map" philosophy
- Dismisses the hostel owner's offer of a tour
- Flirts badly with anyone who will listen

## The Day They Die

### Morning
Wakes up at 2 AM needing to use the bathroom. Confidently sets off without his phone flashlight. Gets turned around. Keeps going, certain he'll find it. Doesn't.

### Their Last Normal Moment
Standing at a fork in the corridor, choosing left because "left always works." It doesn't work. He never finds his way back.

### Final Thoughts
"This can't be happening. I know where I am. I always know where I am. Just... one more turn. One more. One more. Please. Somebody. Help."

### Last Words
"Help! HELP! SOMEBODY!" (echoing off ancient walls, heard by no one)
